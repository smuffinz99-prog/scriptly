How to write a viral TikTok script

The most important part of any TikTok video is the first 3 seconds — the hook. If you don't stop the scroll immediately, the algorithm will stop showing your video. A great TikTok script is built on a proven hook formula, a clear script structure, and a strong call to action.

8 proven TikTok hook formulas

🤔 Curiosity Gap

"I discovered a secret that nobody in [niche] talks about..." — makes them watch to the end.

🔥 Hot Take

"Unpopular opinion: most [niche] advice is wrong" — floods comments and triggers the algorithm.

📖 Personal Story

"Two years ago I knew nothing about [niche]. Here's what happened." — builds trust and following.

✅ Tutorial

"How to [topic] in 3 simple steps" — drives saves, the most powerful TikTok signal.

📊 Statistic

"9 out of 10 [audience] make this mistake" — stops scrolling and demands attention.

⚠️ Warning

"Stop doing [thing] right now if you're [audience]" — fear-of-missing-out that converts.

😩 Relatable POV

"POV: You're [audience] trying to figure out [topic]" — earns duets and massive shares.

📋 Power List

"3 [niche] tips every [audience] needs to know" — drives saves and repeat content.

TikTok script structure that works

Hook (0–3 sec): Stop the scroll. This is the most important part. Use one of the 8 proven formulas above.

Setup (3–10 sec): Establish why this matters to your specific audience. Don't waste time on introductions.

Body (10–50 sec): Deliver the value. Keep it tight — one idea per second. Never pad.

CTA (last 3 sec): Tell them exactly what to do: follow, comment, share, or save. Pick one action.
